Block Island Glass Floats

Each year since 2012, glass floats made by artists Eben Horton, Jennifer Nauck and the glassblowers at The Glass Station Studio and Gallery in Wakefield, Rhode Island have been hidden on Block Island as part of the Block Island Glass Floats project. Each float is individually numbered and dated. Most of these floats are made from clear glass, but each year the first several glass floats (same as the number of years after 2000, so for 2023 there will be 23) are extra fancy floats with bright colors and decorations. Anyone who finds a glass float is asked to register the year and number on their float and where on the island they found it. This interactive map uses that information to show where the regular and fancy floats have been found on the island each year, and how many have been found in each area.
